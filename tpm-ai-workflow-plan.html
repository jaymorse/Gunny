01

Guiding principles

Five rules that keep AI in its lane — a fast analyst that assembles, never the person who signs off.

P1

Automate the assembly, own the judgment

AI drafts the report and pulls the metrics. You decide what's red, what to escalate, and what to tell a VP.

P2

One source of truth in, artifacts out

Keep program data in one place. Point AI at it to generate the deck, the exec email, the RAID log — never re-key by hand.

P3

Templatize once, reuse forever

A TPM's outputs repeat weekly. Build a prompt for each recurring artifact so week N is a 30-second refresh.

P4

Verify before you send

AI-drafted numbers and commitments get a human check. Strong first draft — not signed-off truth.

P5

Respect data handling

Confirm what program and customer data may enter which system per Oracle policy before wiring up connectors.

03

Workflow playbook

Nine workflows with the exact prompt to use. Copy a prompt, point it at your program's data, and refine the output.

W1

The morning brief

Replaces: 20–30 min of scanning mail, calendar, and chat to reconstruct what's going on.

MailCalendarSlack / Teams
Daily prompt
Give me my morning brief. Summarize unread mail from the last 16 hours grouped by program, list today's meetings with what each is about, surface anything where someone is waiting on me or flagged a blocker, and give me a prioritized top-5 action list.

Level up: schedule it to run automatically each weekday so the brief is waiting for you.

W2

Meeting prep

Replaces: digging through old notes before a call.

CalendarDrive / DocsMail history
Prompt
My next meeting is [program X sync]. Pull the last decisions and open action items for that program, who owns what, and anything unresolved from the previous session. Give me a half-page prep sheet.
W3

Meeting notes → decisions → actions

Replaces: note-taking and the "who agreed to what" reconstruction afterward — the biggest TPM time sink.

Transcription (Fireflies)MailTracker
Prompt
From this meeting transcript, give me: (1) a 5-bullet summary, (2) every decision made, (3) an action-item table with owner and due date, (4) any risks or dependencies raised. Then draft a follow-up email to attendees.

Confirm owners and dates, paste actions into your tracker, send the follow-up.

W4

Inbox & chat drafting

Replaces: writing the same status nudges, cross-team asks, and "circling back" notes all day.

MailSlack / Teams
Prompt
Draft a Slack message to #program-x asking each workstream lead for their RAG status and top risk by EOD Thursday. Then summarize this 40-message thread into the decision, who's blocked, and what I need to do.

Save a short style note ("concise, direct, lead with the ask") the assistant reuses to keep drafts in your voice.

W5

The weekly status report

Flagship

Replaces: the 1–2 hours every week spent assembling status from many sources into the standard format.

Tracker (xlsx)Meeting notesDocs / Slides
Weekly prompt
Using the program tracker at [link] and this week's meeting notes, generate our standard weekly status report: overall RAG, per-workstream status with the delta since last week, milestones hit and missed, top 3 risks, and asks for leadership. Match the format of last week's report.

You set the RAG colors, add the narrative and the "so what," and mark escalations. Once the format stabilizes, save it as a reusable template — week N becomes run → review → send.

W6

Risk / RAID log maintenance

Replaces: manually reconciling what changed in the risk register.

Risk registerPrior week
Prompt
Compare this week's risk register to last week's. What's new, what aged without movement, what closed? Flag any risk open over 2 weeks with no mitigation owner, and suggest a mitigation for each new one.
W7

Data & metrics work

Replaces: cleaning exports, building the same charts, computing rollups by hand.

Spreadsheet (xlsx)DatavizWolfram
Prompt
From this defect export: give me open vs. closed by week, average age of open blockers, and which team is trending worse. Then build a burn-down and a defect-trend chart I can drop into the exec deck.

Always spot-check AI-computed figures against the raw source before they go in a report.

W8

Executive summaries & decks

Replaces: translating detailed program state into a VP-ready one-pager or deck.

Slides (pptx)Docs (docx / pdf)
Prompt
Turn this weekly status into a 5-slide exec update: one-line program health, milestone timeline, top 3 risks with mitigations, key decision needed, and the ask. Executive tone — outcomes, not activity.
W9

Cross-program search & recall

Replaces: "where did we decide that?" archaeology across docs and chat.

DriveSlack / Teams
Prompt
Search my Drive and Slack for the decision on the [auth migration] rollback plan and tell me what we agreed and who owned it.
04

Rollout — crawl, walk, run

A sequenced four-week adoption path. Prove value on low-risk work first; automate the flagship last.

1
Week 1

Crawl — prove value on low-risk work

  • Connect mail, calendar, and drive/docs (within Oracle data policy).
  • Adopt two workflows only: the morning brief and notes → action items.
  • Goal: feel the time savings on the two highest-frequency tasks.
2
Weeks 2–3

Walk — attack the flagship

  • Consolidate program data into one tracker.
  • Build the weekly status report workflow and run it for a real cycle.
  • Add inbox/chat drafting and meeting prep to the daily habit.
3
Week 4

Run — templatize & automate

  • Turn weekly status, exec update, and RAID diff into saved templates.
  • Schedule the morning brief to run automatically each weekday.
  • Add metrics/dataviz for the recurring charts.
∞
Ongoing

Compound — make it stick

  • Every time you do a repetitive task twice, template it.
  • Keep a personal prompt library of what works for your programs.

06

Guardrails & data handling

Non-negotiable

  • Confirm Oracle policy on what program, customer, and financial data may enter each AI system before wiring up connectors. Prefer approved/internal AI for anything sensitive.
  • Human-in-the-loop on anything outbound — every AI-drafted email, exec number, and commitment gets a review before it leaves.
  • No unverified numbers in reports. AI assembles; you verify against source.
  • Least-privilege connectors. Grant only the access each workflow needs.
  • Keep an audit trail. Reports and decisions live in the system of record, not a chat thread.
